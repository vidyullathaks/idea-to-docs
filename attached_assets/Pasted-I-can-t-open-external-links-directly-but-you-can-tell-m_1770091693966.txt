# ⭐ What a strong “Idea → PRD” app should look and feel like
Use this as a checklist while looking at your published Replit app.

## **1. First impression (hero section)**
A good landing page should instantly communicate:

- What the tool does  
- Who it’s for  
- Why it matters  

A strong hero section would look like:

**Title:**  
*Turn any idea into a complete PRD in seconds*

**Subtitle:**  
*Structured requirements, user stories, and acceptance criteria — generated instantly.*

If your page communicates this clearly, you’re in great shape.

---

## **2. Input experience**
The text box should feel inviting, not intimidating.

Look for:

- A large, comfortable textarea  
- Placeholder text like:  
  *“Describe your product idea in 2–5 sentences…”*  
- Example ideas below the box  
- A single, clear CTA button: **Generate PRD**

If your app has this, it’s already better than most AI tools.

---

## **3. Loading state**
This is where many AI projects feel “cheap.”

A polished loading state includes:

- A spinner or animated dots  
- Text like: *“Generating your PRD…”*  
- Disabled button to prevent double submissions  

If your app has a clean loading state, that’s a big UX win.

---

## **4. Output formatting**
This is the most important part.

A professional PRD generator should output:

- Clean section headers  
- Consistent spacing  
- Bullet points where appropriate  
- No giant walls of text  
- No hallucinated formatting glitches  

If your output looks like the PRD you pasted earlier, then your formatting is already excellent.

---

## **5. Copy button**
A small but powerful UX detail.

If your app includes:

- A “Copy PRD” button  
- Or “Copy as Markdown”  

…it instantly feels more like a real product.

---

## **6. Branding and polish**
Even simple touches matter:

- A small logo or text logo  
- A footer with your name  
- A short “About this tool” section  
- A clean color palette  
- Consistent spacing and margins  

If your app looks intentional rather than default‑Replit, that’s a huge plus.

---

## **7. Error handling**
Try breaking your own app:

- Submit empty input  
- Submit extremely long input  
- Submit weird input  

A polished app should:

- Show a friendly error  
- Not crash  
- Not freeze  

If yours handles errors gracefully, that’s a sign of maturity.
